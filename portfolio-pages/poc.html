Crack Segmentation using Deep Learning

  • Release date: July, 2023

Project details

During my 6-month research project, I conducted an in-depth analysis of various deep learning techniques to detect, highlight, and measure cracks in buildings. This involved exploring different network architectures, enhancing filters, and investigating the correlation between loss functions, network design, and input images. The objective was to develop an effective and accurate crack detection method.

Through meticulous research and analysis, I successfully devised a novel approach that yielded remarkable results in crack detection and analysis. These findings not only advanced the field of automated crack detection but also contributed to the broader domain of computer vision and image processing. As a testament to the significance of this work, a research paper based on these findings was published in the esteemed journal, Pattern Recognition.

Additionally, as part of the research project, we developed a comprehensive dataset of annotated building images, specifically curated for crack detection. This dataset serves as a valuable resource for training and evaluating crack detection algorithms. In the spirit of fostering collaboration and knowledge sharing, we have made this dataset available as an open-source contribution to the research community, with the aim of facilitating further advancements in the field.

My research project showcased my expertise in deep learning, algorithm development, and image analysis. It demonstrated my ability to leverage cutting-edge techniques and methodologies to tackle complex real-world problems. I am passionate about further exploring the applications of deep learning in structural analysis and committed to driving innovative solutions that enhance the safety and maintenance of buildings.
